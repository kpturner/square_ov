# Officers

## What this feature does

The feature shows you the order of your procession based on the officers you have entered for your official visit. It will also show you the list of ranks in order for a visual reference. The procession order is entirely dependent on the officer information you have provided. If you get this wrong, then your procession may also be wrong.

You can print the procession in printer friendly format if you wish. Suitable gaps are provided on the printout for manual changes to be made if you wish to do so.

## What are the options I can take for each procession?

Above the procession there are some special options:

- Procession total - This shows you the total number of officers in your procession. This is useful for the DC to know how many officers are in the procession, and to ensure that the procession is not too long or too short.
- Align active wardens? - This feature only applies to Craft processions where it is normal to keep the active wardens in line with each other if they are both attending the official visit.
- Reverse steward order? - Active provincial stewards are normally placed towards the front of the procession in order of their active officer number. You can opt to reverse this order.
- Active DCs at the front? - If you have two or more active DCs in the procession, it is normal for them to be at the front of the procession (i.e. in front of the Stewards). This option will do that for you.
- If the previous option is selected, you can also choose to include active Deputies atthe front.
- Also if you select "Active DCs at front" you can opt to include Grand Officer DCs at the front.
- Carpet Capacity - This enables you to define how many officers the carpet can accomodate in a column. If the number of officers exceed this number, then the system will provide a second procession order below the first one entitled "Split columns on carpet". Here the columns are split into "inner/outer south" and "inner/outer north". This is useful for large processions where the carpet cannot accomodate all the officers in a single column. In this scenario, there is an additional option to force all the senior officers are to the rear of the procession across all four columns, but the recommendation is to stick with the default provided.

---

## When to use it

Use this option during your briefing to form up your procession. You can also use it to check that your procession is correct before the official visit takes place.
